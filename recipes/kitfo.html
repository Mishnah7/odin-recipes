Kitfo

Description

Kitfo is a traditional Ethiopian dish made from raw minced beef, seasoned with spices and clarified butter. It is often served with a side of greens and is a popular choice for special occasions.

This dish is known for its rich flavor and is typically enjoyed with injera, a sourdough flatbread that complements the taste of the meat.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound raw minced beef (preferably lean)
  • 1/4 cup clarified butter (niter kibbeh)
  • 1-2 tablespoons mitmita (Ethiopian spice blend)
  • Salt to taste
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh greens (like gomen or collard greens) for serving
  • Injera (Ethiopian flatbread) for serving

Steps:

  1. Prepare the Niter Kibbeh:
    • If you don't have niter kibbeh, you can make it by melting unsalted butter and adding spices like garlic, ginger, and cardamom. Simmer for about 10-15 minutes, then strain.
  2. Mix the Beef:
    • In a bowl, combine the raw minced beef with the clarified butter. Mix well to ensure the beef is coated.
  3. Add Spices:
    • Add mitmita and salt to the beef mixture. Adjust the amount of mitmita based on your spice preference. Mix thoroughly.
  4. Serve:
    • Traditionally, Kitfo is served raw, but you can lightly sear it in a pan if you prefer it cooked.
    • Serve the Kitfo on a plate with a side of chopped greens and injera.
  5. Enjoy:
    • Use pieces of injera to scoop up the Kitfo and greens.

Notes:

  • Mitmita is a spicy Ethiopian chili powder. If you can't find it, you can substitute with a mix of cayenne pepper and other spices.
  • Niter Kibbeh can be purchased at Ethiopian grocery stores or made at home.
  • Kitfo can also be served with a side of ayib (Ethiopian cottage cheese) for a milder flavor.
